HUNDRED ISLANDS

Location

History

ACTIVITIES TO DO

Hundred Islands National Park is best explored by island hopping. Visitors can swim, snorkel, and kayak around the many islets. Popular stops include Governor's Island for its view deck and zip line, Quezon Island for its picnic areas, and Children's Island for its family-friendly shallow waters.

ROUTE AND TIPS

To get there, take a 5-6 hour bus from Manila to Alaminos City, then a tricycle to Lucap Wharf. The best time to visit is the dry season (November to May). Plan your tour early as schedules depend on the weather, and remember to bring eco-friendly essentials.

THINGS TO DO

Mt. Pulag is known for its famous "sea of clouds" sunrise. Trekkers can choose from several trails, ranging from beginner-friendly to challenging, that pass through diverse landscapes like pine forests and dwarf bamboo.

SAGADA SUMAGUING CAVE

Location

History

WHY VISIT SUMAGUING CAVE?

Sumaguing Cave is Sagada's largest and most iconic cave, famous for its incredible limestone formations. The adventure involves a challenging descent, crawling through tunnels, and wading in icy waters, making it a must for nature and adventure lovers.

ROUTE AND TIPS

Travel involves a bus from Manila to Baguio or Banaue, then a transfer to Sagada. Hire a licensed guide from the town, visit in the dry season (November-April), and wear sturdy, non-slip shoes for the wet and slippery cave interior.

VIGAN

WHAT TO DO IN VIGAN?

A visit centers on the iconic Calle Crisologo, a cobblestone street where you can ride a kalesa, explore ancestral houses, and try local food like Vigan longganisa. Don't miss the Bantay Bell Tower for views and the Plaza Salcedo dancing fountain show at night.

HISTORY OF VIGAN

Vigan is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a exceptionally well-preserved Spanish colonial town from the 16th century. Its unique architecture and cobblestone streets reflect a rich blend of Filipino, Chinese, and European cultural influences.

ROUTE AND TIPS

Vigan is an 8-10 hour bus ride from Manila. The best time to visit is the dry season (November-April). Wear comfortable shoes for walking, bring cash, and consider staying overnight to fully experience the city's historic charm by day and its lively atmosphere by night.
